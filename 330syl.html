Syllabus – Abstract Algebra I

MATH 330, Fall 2024 (28003), 3 Credit Hours
MWF 11:00AM - 11:50AM, GH 204, On Campus

Course Description: This is a first semester proofs-based undegraduate abstract algebra course, aimed primarily at MSCS majors. Abstract algebra is one of the main pillars of modern mathematics, and is widely used across other branches of mathematics including geometry, number theory, and cryptography. It also has many real-world applications in fields such as computer science, physics, engineering, and economics. In this course, we will explore the most fundamental structures of algebra including groups, rings, fields. Students will be expected to read, write, and comprehend proofs, as well as to do problems involving both computations and proofs.

Prerequisite: Grade of C or better in Math 215.

Course Goals and Student Learning Objectives:

  1. Identify and define basic examples of groups, rings, and fields, and explain their fundamental properties and structures.

  2. Examine the properties and differences between various algebraic structures, and compare and contrast them.

  3. Build examples and non-examples to illustrate abstract definitions and theorem statements.

  4. Construct clear and logical mathematical proofs, and critically assess the validity of proofs and arguments.

  5. Analyze problems, formulate conjectures, and generalize results within the context of abstract algebra.

  6. Participate in interactive class components to reinforce lecture material and discuss mathematical arguments and proofs.

Text (required): Abstract Algebra: An Introduction by Thomas W. Hungerford.

Weekly Schedule: We’ll cover approximately the first eight chapters of the text, as detailed below in the tentative schedule of the sections to be covered for each lecture. To be prepared for each class, make sure to read the appropriate section of the text before coming to lecture.

Week 1

M The Division Algorithm (Section 1.1 & Appendix A), W Divisibility (Section 1.2 & Appendix C) & HW 0, F Primes and Unique Factorization (Section 1.3)

Week 2

M No Class (Labor Day), W Congruence and Congruence Classes (Section 2.1 & Appendix D) & HW 1, F Modular Arithmetic (Section 2.2 & Appendix E)

Week 3

M The Structure of ℤp\mathbb{Z}_p (pp prime) and ℤn\mathbb{Z}_n (Section 2.3), W Public-Key Cryptography (Chapter 13) & HW 2, F Definition and Examples of Rings (Section 3.1)

Week 4

M Basic Properties of Rings (Section 3.2), W Isomorphisms and Homomorphisms (Section 3.3) & HW 3, F Polynomial Arithmetic and the Division Algorithm (Section 4.1)

Week 5

M Divisibility in F[x]F[x] (Section 4.2), W Irreducibles and Unique Factorization (Section 4.3) & HW 4, F Polynomial Functions, Roots, and Reducibility (Section 4.4)

Week 6

M Congruence in F[x]F[x] and Congruence Classes (Section 5.1), W Congruence-Class Arithmetic (Section 5.2) & HW 5, F The Structure of F[x]/(p(x))F[x]/(p(x)) When p(x)p(x) is Irreducible

Week 7

M Review for Midterm, W In class Midterm Exam, F Ideals and Congruence (Section 6.1)

Week 8

M Quotient Rings and Homomorphisms (Section 6.2), W The Structure of R/IR/I when II is Prime or Maximal (Section 6.3) & HW 6, F Euclidean Domains (Section 10.1)

Week 9

M Principal Ideal Domains and Unique Factorization Domains (Section 10.2), W Definition and Examples of Groups (Section 7.1) & HW 7, F Sets and Functions (Appendix B)

Week 10

M Definition and Examples of Groups (Section 7.1), W Basic Properties of Groups (Section 7.2) & HW 8, F Basic Properties of Groups (Section 7.2)

Week 11

M Subgroups (Section 7.3), W Subgroups (Section 7.3) & HW 9, F Isomorphisms and Homomorphisms (Section 7.4)

Week 12

M Isomorphisms and Homomorphisms (Section 7.4), W The Symmetric and Alternating Groups (Section 7.5) & HW 10, F The Symmetric and Alternating Groups (Section 7.5)

Week 13

M Congruence and Lagrange’s Theorem (Section 8.1), W Normal Subgroups (Section 8.2) & HW 11, F Quotient Groups (Section 8.3)

Week 14

M Quotient Groups and Homomorphisms (Section 8.4), W & F No Class (Thanksgiving)

Week 15

M Direct Products (Section 9.1) & HW 12, W Finite Abelian Groups (Section 9.2), F Review for the (take home) final
